# Business Research Method

Use this reference before writing international business recommendations.

## Goal

Do not produce generic language or market advice.

First determine:

- what the store actually sells
- what use case the products solve
- whether the category is travel-led, home-led, gifting-led, seasonal, or premium-led
- which countries match that use case

Then convert that into practical Shopify advice.

## Minimum Store Profiling

Use all of these before writing business advice:

1. `shop.name`
2. `shop.description`
3. visible collection names or navigation labels
4. visible product names from the storefront
5. at least one product-detail page when possible

Output a short internal profile such as:

- `dog-first pet travel brand`
- `premium compact pet carrier brand`
- `car-travel safety and comfort brand`

## What To Look For In Products

Infer the business type from:

- target pet: dog, cat, or mixed
- scenario: car travel, flight travel, walking, recovery, home use
- price band: entry, mid, premium
- benefit: safety, comfort, portability, style, anxiety reduction, cleanliness
- buyer type: frequent traveler, urban pet parent, family with car, gift buyer

## External Research Minimum

Before making country-opportunity claims, gather at least `3` external references.

Prefer these source types:

- industry associations
- government trade reports
- national pet population surveys
- category reports from official or semi-official institutions

Avoid building the whole recommendation on:

- generic ecommerce blogs
- ungated SEO listicles
- low-authority shopping guides

## Freshness Rules

Prefer the newest credible source available.

- First look for the latest official or primary-source publication.
- If a newer source exists, do not anchor the recommendation on an older one without saying why.
- If the latest primary source is older, say that the primary source is older and pair it with a newer directional source only as supporting context.
- When the user asks for current or latest recommendations, explicitly compare years and keep the newest valid source.

## Suggested Search Paths

Start with these search patterns and adapt them to the store category:

- `<country> pet ownership official`
- `<country> pet market report official`
- `<country> pet travel trend`
- `<country> dog ownership survey`
- `<country> cat ownership survey`
- `<country> ecommerce pet category growth`
- `<country> premium pet product trend`
- `<country> pet carrier travel demand`
- `<country> pet safety car travel`

Always include at least one freshness-oriented query when the recommendation depends on market timing:

- `latest <country> pet market official`
- `2026 <country> pet market report`
- `latest <country> pet ownership survey`
- `latest <country> ecommerce pet trend`

For government or trade sources:

- `site:gov <country> pet market`
- `site:gc.ca pet food <country>`
- `site:fda.gov pet`
- `site:usda.gov pet market <country>`
- `site:agriculture.canada.ca pet <country>`

For category associations:

- `site:americanpetproducts.org pet ownership`
- `site:europeanpetfood.org statistics`
- `site:ukpetfood.org pet population`

## Reasoning Framework

Do not rank countries only by population.

Score candidate markets using:

1. pet ownership or pet population density
2. category fit
3. ecommerce readiness
4. product-use context
5. localization difficulty

### Category Fit Examples

For pet travel and carrier brands, look for:

- high dog and cat ownership
- frequent driving or travel-with-pet behavior
- urban apartment living if carriers or compact products matter
- premiumization and humanization of pets
- strong demand for safety, convenience, and washable materials

For cat-heavy brands, weigh:

- cat population
- indoor-living patterns
- small-home usage contexts

For dog car products, weigh:

- car travel relevance
- family-pet ownership
- safety-led buying behavior

## Output Rules

The business suggestion section must include:

1. `Store profile`
2. `Priority countries`
3. `Why these countries fit`
4. `Practical impact on Markets / languages / pricing / policies`

Use plain language.

Explicitly label:

- `Evidence`: directly supported by references
- `Inference`: reasonable category judgment from the combined evidence

## What Not To Do

Do not say:

- `Germany is good because Europe is important`
- `Add French because French is common`
- `Try more languages for SEO`

Instead say things like:

- `This brand is dog-travel-first, so countries with strong dog ownership and road-trip pet behavior matter more than countries that are merely large.`
- `Because the carrier products emphasize compact, breathable, cabin-friendly design, dense urban markets with smaller homes and indoor pets are a better fit than broad untargeted expansion.`

Do not:

- hardcode any live merchant example into this reference
- store any real merchant profile in the skill files
- assume the category from web search before checking Admin API or storefront evidence
